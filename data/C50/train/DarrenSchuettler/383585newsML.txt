Canada's financial markets gave an approving nod to Finance Minister Paul Martin's stay the course budget on Tuesday, with some analysts expecting a modest rally on Wednesday. In what is expected to be an election year, the Liberal government said it will maintain pressure on the deficit while offering minor tax cuts and new spending. Economists said Martin's assumptions on interest rates and economic growth were prudent and should enable the government to beat its fiscal targets.
