Hong Kong Dragon Airlines Dragonair is increasing its flights to Sendai in Japan from October to six a week from the present four, the carrier's chief operating officer Philip Chen said in a statement. Our services to Hiroshima and Sendai are a good example of how Dragonair is spreading out and serving the region. Since we started Hiroshima in and Sendai in both have been very successful and we are increasing frequencies on both these routes to cope with demand, he added One of Dragonair's current fleet of seven Airbus Industrie A will be used for the extra frequency, a spokeswoman added. On October , Airbus said Dragonair had signed a contract an order for two more A s, plus options for five others along with the lease of another Airbus A . The new planes will be used to cope with further route expansion to China and other Asian destinations, especially when Hong Kong's new round the clock airport opens in months time, industry sources said.
