Apple Computer Inc. will unveil Monday a new family of portable PowerBook computers to fill a critical gap in its product line. The new PowerBook family, scheduled to be sold in mid November with prices starting at , , will feature a built in CD ROM drive and a bigger screen. The models are designed to appeal to small office users and students, Apple said. Until now, the lack of a CD ROM drive in its portables has held back Apple in competing with offerings from International Business Machines Corp., Compaq Computer Corp. and Dell Computer Corp., analysts said. The new family isn't going to put them in the leadership role, but it's going to get them back in the game, said Bruce Steven, an analyst at market researcher International Data Corp. Apple said it also plans to introduce another new series of PowerBooks in the first half of the with more advanced multimedia and communication capabilities. The company did not release any more specifications. Apple has been scrambling to retool the PowerBook line. The predecessor to the new model, the PowerBook , was recalled last spring because of several defects including a faulty AC power connector and a display casing that could easily crack. The PowerBook will replace that family. The new machines will be based on a mid range PowerPC microprocessor. The faster microprocessor, the CD ROM drive and bigger screen make the comparable in price and performance to top selling models from other vendors, but the will have a tough time competing with low priced offerings from second tier PC manufacturers, Steven said.
